About Us

Beginning: Air Jordan 1 (1985)

The Air Jordan legacy began with the revolutionary Air Jordan 1, introduced in 1985. Designed by Peter Moore, this groundbreaking sneaker disrupted the status quo with its bold colorways and innovative design. Its rebellious nature, famously defying NBA uniform regulations, turned heads and set a new precedent for what a basketball shoe could represent. The Air Jordan 1 quickly became more than just a performance shoe; it symbolized individuality and defiance, establishing the Air Jordan brand as a powerful force in both basketball and fashion.

Evolution: Air Jordan 2 (1986)

The Air Jordan 2, released in 1986, marked a significant evolution in the Air Jordan line. Designed by Bruce Kilgore, this model was distinguished by its luxurious Italian leather upper and minimalist design. Innovation: Air Jordan 3 (1988)

The Air Jordan 3, launched in 1988 and designed by Tinker Hatfield, represented a major leap forward in sneaker innovation. This model introduced visible Air cushioning and the iconic elephant print detailing, setting new benchmarks for both functionality and style. The Air Jordan 3 not only enhanced Michael Jordan's performance but also cemented the Air Jordan brand's place in popular culture. Its forward-thinking design and cultural impact ensured that the Air Jordan 3 would become a timeless icon in the world of sneakers.
